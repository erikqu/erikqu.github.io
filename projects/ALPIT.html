Adversarial Learning for Personalized Image Tagging

Over the summer of 2018 I had the amazing opportunity to do research at the University of Central Florida at CRCV under Dr. Mubarak Shah and Dr. Yogesh Rawat. We developed a model here shown below that would take past user history into account when generating image tags for a given image. This in turn gives us better image labels. I will first delve into some highlights about the project and then get into specifics about the model below.

  • We made use of NUS-WIDE and YFCC100M, YFCC100M itself being ~1TB of image/user data.
  • Since these datasets are based on Flickr, the user data was retrieved manually for every user.
  • This is the first time deep learning is used to condense user preferences to generate image labels.
  • Provides scalable solution for generating tags using deep learning.
  • Adversarial learning used in the process, making use of a discriminator to generate better and better tags.
  • We leverage an AE to encode the user vectors, which is then combined with intermediate output of ResNet-50.
  • Everything here was done mainly in Keras, although some of our earlier models made use of Caffe.
  • Accuracy of this model in Top-K, K@3, is 91% on NUS-WIDE (state of the art)
  • Submitted to ACM-MM 2019
